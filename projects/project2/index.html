Project 2: Persuasive and Deceptive Visualizations

Proposition

The NYPD has increasingly failed to take action in response to civilian complaints.

FOR the Proposition

Visualization 1

Design Decisions and Rationale:

  • Used bold colors to differientiate the trends and make the trend feel urgent.
  • Excluded data after 2020 to avoid incomplete results.
  • Framed the title to focus on failure to act.

AGAINST the Proposition

Visualization 2

Design Decisions and Rationale:

  • Chose green color to signal growth and control.
  • Excluded 2019 data, which showed a major spike in officer arrests to keep the trend stable and support the argument.
  • Thickened the line to make the trend look steadier.
  • Widened the timeline to flatten out sudden changes.
  • Framed the title to show accountability instead of misconduct.

Final Reflection

This project showed that even small design choices, such as color, axis ranges, and timeline cuts, can make a significant difference in a visualization’s message. I learned how easy it is to guide a viewer toward a specific idea just by adjusting what is shown and how it is framed. While it is normal and important to be deliberate with your design choices, it is also important to do it honestly and responsibly. You can effectively guide people to understand your message without hiding important information or being deceptive.
